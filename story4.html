High Crimes & Misdemeanors

Drug arrests in the Bronx have dropped over the last ten years

BY MICHAEL TASHJI

According to data from the New York State Division of Criminal Justice, felony arrests for drug offenses have seen a steep decline, while violent and property arrests have seen little to no change over the same 10-year period.

Misdemeanor drug arrests have also seen a drastic decline, but that trend is true for other misdemeanors in the Bronx including property and other crimes.

DWI arrests, both felony and misdemeanor, have seen little change, and make up a small fraction of overall arrests in the borough.

The drop in felony drug arrests comes at a time when the Bronx, like much of the nation, has seen a resurgence of heroin and opioid use.

Overdoses from heroin, fentanyl and prescription painkillers are on the rise, according to the New York City Office of Chief Medical Examiner. Bronx residents have died from drug overdose at a higher rate than any other borough in the city, especially in the neighborhoods of Hunts Point-Mott Haven, Crotona-Tremont, High Bridge-Morrisania and Fordham-Bronx Park.
