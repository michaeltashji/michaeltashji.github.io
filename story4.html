Bronx Drug Arrests Are Down

Felony and misdemeanor drug arrests have dropped over the last 10 years

BY MICHAEL TASHJI

Felony arrests for drug offenses have seen a steep decline over the last ten years, while violent and property arrests have seen little to no change, according to data from the New York State Division of Criminal Justice. Between 2008 and 2017, the Bronx has seen a 59 percent decrease in the number of felony drug arrests.

The number of misdemeanor drug arrests in the Bronx has also declined over the same 10-year period, representing a 66 percent decrease across the borough.

The drop in drug arrests comes at a time when the Bronx, like much of the nation, has seen an increase of heroin and opioid use.

Heroin, fentanyl and prescription painkiller overdoses are on the rise, according to the New York City Office of Chief Medical Examiner. Bronx residents have died from drug overdoses at a higher rate than any other borough in the city, especially in the neighborhoods of Hunts Point-Mott Haven, Crotona-Tremont, High Bridge-Morrisania and Fordham-Bronx Park.
